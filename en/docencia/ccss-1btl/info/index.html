📊 Assessment criteria

Each term combines progress assessments (at least two) with one comprehensive end-of-term exam. A minimum mark of 3.5 on the comprehensive exam is required to pass; if not reached, the term mark is capped at 4. The subject is cumulative: the 2nd-term comprehensive also includes the 1st-term content, and the 3rd-term comprehensive covers the entire course.

1st term until 28 Nov

U01–U04 · Real numbers, Polynomials, Equations and systems, Inequalities

Progress assessments (≥ 2)60%
1st comprehensive (min. 3.5)40%

2nd term until 27 Feb

U05–U07 · Real functions, Function study and graphing, Introduction to derivatives

Progress assessments (≥ 2)60%
2nd comprehensive (min. 3.5 · cumulative)40%

3rd term until 5 Jun

U08–U10 · Statistics, Probability, Probability distributions

Progress assessments (≥ 2)60%
3rd comprehensive (min. 3.5 · whole course)40%

Final course weighting: 1st 20% · 2nd 30% · 3rd 50% (provided all three terms are passed). If any term remains failed, the subject is failed and a sufficiency exam is taken at the end of the year.
