About me

From an early age I've been fascinated by solving problems. I never cared whether a solution had any practical application — I simply enjoyed thinking, analysing, and getting to the bottom of things. I graduated in Mathematics from the FME at UPC, and also completed a Master's in Applied Mathematics and another in Teacher Training.

I spent 7 years at Escola Meritxell in Mataró as a mathematics teacher for secondary and sixth form, the last two also as Head of Sixth Form Studies. For the past 8 years I have been a teacher at Col·legi Maristes Sants—Les Corts — the same school where I studied from primary through to secondary.

Fifteen years in the classroom led me to rediscover something I had long forgotten: the urge to tackle real problems. One day I came across the Collatz conjecture — a problem that brings together everything I find fascinating about mathematics. And because of that, I am now pursuing a PhD under the supervision of Dr. Jaume Franch Bullich at UPC.
